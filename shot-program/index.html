How it works

Three phases. One rebuilt shot.

A staged progression — because a shot isn't fixed all at once. It's rebuilt in order.

PHASE 1

Discovery

Weeks 1–4

We film your shot, break it down frame by frame, and identify the areas you can improve your shot. Then we build the mechanics — base, hand placement, release — with hands-on reps until the new motion holds.

PHASE 2

Build

Weeks 5–8

Volume with the new shot. Custom workouts assigned and progress measured, so you and your parents can see the numbers move — not just feel like they're moving.

PHASE 3

Range

Weeks 9–12

Game speed, game range. Off the catch, off the move, off the dribble — and game film breakdown so the new shot shows up where it counts: in games.

    FAQ

    Frequently asked.

    Who is this for?

    Athletes in 7th grade and older who are serious about changing their shot. Younger players: we run Jr Shot Camps and individual sessions — ask about those on the call.

    How is this different from Shot Camp?

    Shot Camp is a 3-day introduction to how we think about shooting. The Shot Program is the full transformation — 90 days, 24 coached sessions, video breakdown, and a tracked, staged progression. Camp shows you the system; the Program rebuilds your shot with it.

    What if my athlete plays other sports or has team practice?

    Eight trainings a month is deliberate — it's enough volume to change a shot while leaving room for team practice and games. We'll build the schedule around your athlete's season on the call.

    How do we know it's working?

    Every session is tracked in our training app. You'll see the workouts assigned, the sessions completed, and the numbers moving — plus video of the shot at the start and as it changes. Progress here is measured, not promised.

    What happens after the 90 days?

    Graduates can continue on the Maintenance Plan ($349/mo, optional): custom workouts, tracked metrics, and continued access to the system. Most athletes keep going — a rebuilt shot stays sharp with structured reps.

    Why do I have to book a call to sign up?

    Ten spots per cohort means fit matters more than speed. The call is where we learn about your athlete's shot and goals — and where you get to ask us anything before committing to 90 days.
